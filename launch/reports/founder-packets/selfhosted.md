# Founder Packet: r/selfhosted

Sources: `launch/communities.json` (4bbb63e36ad5), `docs/GROWTH_AUTOMATION_SPEC.md` (129f5da753f7)

## Board Brief

- Title: Self-Hosted
- Audience: People who run home servers, NAS boxes, personal cloud, and homelab services.
- Launch role: Practical operator funnel for future HiveRelay nodes.
- Founder brief: Focus on durable ownership, migration away from SaaS, and what should run at home versus on relays.
- Moderation risk: low

## Deliverables

- Create one owner/moderator identity for r/selfhosted.
- Publish 15 quality starter posts before paid traffic.
- Publish at least 3 open-ended discussion prompts.
- Pin one norms post and exercise the moderation overlay before launch.
- Reply daily during the first launch week so the board does not feel empty.

## Pinned Norms Post Template

```markdown
# Welcome to r/selfhosted

This board is for People who run home servers, NAS boxes, personal cloud, and homelab services.

Bring concrete links, demos, build notes, questions, and careful comparisons.
Keep claims precise: Peerit has no central account, uses signed identities, and gates posting with small proof-of-work.
Do not position this board around illegal content, harassment, doxxing, ban evasion, or "anything goes" moderation.

Read on the gateway. Post through PearBrowser.
```

## Starter Prompt Checklist

- "What is one Self-Hosted workflow that would benefit from no central account or server?"
- "Show a concrete Self-Hosted build, benchmark, migration, or failure report."
- "What should r/selfhosted moderate quickly during launch week?"
- "Where does Peerit's P2P model fit this audience, and where is it still rough?"
